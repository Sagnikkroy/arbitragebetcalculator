What is Arbitrage Betting?

Arbitrage betting is a strategy that capitalizes on differences in odds offered by various bookmakers to guarantee a profit regardless of the outcome of an event. This approach involves placing bets on all possible outcomes of a sporting event or other wagerable activities in such a way that the combined odds ensure a return greater than the total amount invested. By leveraging discrepancies in the odds set by different sportsbooks, bettors can create a situation where their potential returns outweigh their stakes, effectively securing a risk-free profit. This technique requires careful calculation and prompt execution, as the odds and available opportunities can change rapidly.

say there’s a tennis match between Player X and Player Y. You find the following odds from two different bookmakers:

Bookmaker 1 offers odds of 2.00 for Player X to win. Bookmaker 2 offers odds of 2.10 for Player Y to win. Here’s how you can use these odds to guarantee a profit:

Determine Your Total Investment:

Decide how much you want to invest in total. For this example, let’s say you choose $100.

Calculate the Stake Amounts:

To ensure a profit, you need to place different amounts on each outcome based on the odds. For Player X, you should bet approximately $48.00. For Player Y, you should bet approximately $52.00. The amounts are calculated so that no matter who wins, the total returns will be more than your total investment, guaranteeing a profit.

Place Your Bets:

Bet $48.00 on Player X at Bookmaker 1 with odds of 2.00. Bet $52.00 on Player Y at Bookmaker 2 with odds of 2.10.

Calculate Your Returns:

If Player X wins, you’ll get $48.00 * 2.00 = $96.00. Since you staked $48.00, your profit will be $48.00. If Player Y wins, you’ll get $52.00 * 2.10 = $109.20. Since you staked $52.00, your profit will be $57.20. In both scenarios, you make a profit because of the difference in the odds between the two bookmakers. This is how arbitrage betting allows you to ensure a profit by covering all possible outcomes of an event.
